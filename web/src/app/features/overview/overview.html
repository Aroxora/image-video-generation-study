the key idea

The “next-frame prediction” intuition is backwards.

It's tempting to imagine a model generating frame 1, then predicting frame 2 from it, and so on. For the high-quality systems — Sora, Veo, Kling, Runway, Stable Video Diffusion — that's mostly not what happens. They generate the entire clip at once by denoising, so the model sees all frames simultaneously while generating. That's exactly why motion stays coherent.

True autoregressive next-frame prediction does exist (and is resurging for long / streaming video) — it's just historically been weaker, because errors compound frame to frame. Two families, two mental models.

two families

Denoise the whole thing — or predict tokens in sequence

A

Diffusion

denoises the whole clip at once
  • Start from pure Gaussian noise; predict-and-subtract noise over many steps.
  • All frames are present every step → temporal coherence.
  • Text steers continuously via cross-attention, not by being generated.
  • The engine behind most of the impressive results you've seen.

B

Autoregressive

predicts tokens one at a time
  • Tokenize video into discrete codes with a VQ-VAE.
  • A plain Transformer predicts the next token — exactly like an LLM.
  • Text and frame tokens truly interleave in one sequence.
  • Elegant and unified; closing the quality gap with hybrids.
